# Aegis SCC6 Outreach Email

## Use Case
This is a short outreach email for community college leaders, workforce program directors, or grant development staff who could lead an SCC6 proposal.

## Version 1: Cold Outreach

Subject: Possible SCC6 partnership for AI infrastructure workforce training

Hi [Name],

I’m reaching out because I believe [Institution] could be a strong fit for the U.S. Department of Labor’s Strengthening Community Colleges Training Grants Round 6 opportunity, and I think Aegis-GPU could strengthen that kind of proposal as a training partner.

Aegis-GPU is a browser-based AI infrastructure incident training platform designed to help learners build practical troubleshooting judgment across GPU hardware, CUDA stack, storage, networking, and orchestration scenarios. The platform emphasizes evidence-first reasoning, action safety, and measurable readiness rather than content delivery alone.

I think this could fit well inside a short-term, stackable pathway in AI infrastructure operations, platform support, or adjacent technical workforce training. We already have guided labs, reasoning scorecards, progress reporting, and representative browser-proof validation across key product flows.

If SCC6 or a related workforce-training concept is on your radar, I’d welcome a short conversation to see whether Aegis could serve as the simulation, assessment, and learner-readiness layer in a community-college-led proposal.

I can send a one-page summary and pilot outline if useful.

Best,  
[Founder Name]  
[Title]  
[Organization]  
[Email]  
[Phone]

## Version 2: Slightly Stronger Ask

Subject: Could Aegis-GPU support an SCC6 concept at [Institution]?

Hi [Name],

I’m contacting you because [Institution] appears well positioned for the Department of Labor’s Strengthening Community Colleges Training Grants Round 6 competition, and I’d like to explore whether Aegis-GPU could help strengthen an application.

Aegis-GPU is a browser-based training platform for AI infrastructure troubleshooting. It gives learners guided incident practice across GPU, CUDA, NCCL, scheduler, storage, and networking scenarios, with reasoning scorecards and measurable progress outputs. The goal is to improve real troubleshooting readiness, not just content exposure.

I believe Aegis could support a college-led short-term pathway tied to AI infrastructure operations, technical support, or platform workforce preparation, especially where the proposal needs a stronger hands-on training and evidence layer.

Would you be open to a brief call next week? I can also send a concise summary, pilot plan, and current grant-fit memo in advance.

Best,  
[Founder Name]

## Send-To Roles
Best first contacts:
- dean of workforce development
- dean of continuing education
- STEM division chair
- grants office director
- vice president for workforce or academic innovation
- program lead for IT, cloud, cybersecurity, or advanced technical training

## Guidance
- Keep the first email short.
- Do not attach too much on first touch unless requested.
- Offer the one-page summary and pilot outline as the next step.
- If you have any local employer or regional workforce angle, add it in one sentence.
