# Aegis Target Opportunity: DOL Strengthening Community Colleges Training Grants Round 6

## Recommended Target
U.S. Department of Labor  
Strengthening Community Colleges Training Grants (Round 6)  
FOA-ETA-26-40

## Why This Is The Best Current Near-Term Fit
This is the strongest currently identified fit for Aegis because the program is explicitly focused on:
- community colleges
- short-term, high-quality workforce training
- employer alignment
- statewide workforce-system integration
- portable and stackable career-pathway design

That matches Aegis more closely than a generic innovation or startup grant because Aegis is best framed today as a workforce-readiness and technical training platform.

## Current Official Facts
Based on current U.S. Department of Labor sources:
- Issue date: February 17, 2026
- Closing date: May 20, 2026
- Total funding announced: $65 million
- Individual awards: up to approximately $11 million

## Key Fit Language From Official Sources
The Department of Labor states that Round 6 will fund community colleges with a focus on building capacity for short-term training through Workforce Pell Grants and strengthening integration with the larger state workforce system. The DOL news release also states that programs must be portable and stackable along a career pathway and meet employer hiring requirements in in-demand industries.

## Important Eligibility Constraint
Aegis is likely not the natural prime applicant for this opportunity unless it is housed inside an eligible community-college structure.

Best current application posture:
- community college, statewide community college system, or college consortium as prime
- Aegis as the training platform, curriculum, or implementation partner

This matters because the opportunity is framed around community colleges as the funded lead entities.

## Best Aegis Position In An SCC6 Proposal
Aegis should be positioned as:
- the incident-training and troubleshooting-readiness platform powering short-term AI infrastructure training
- a way to make technical learning more measurable through reasoning scorecards and pilot evidence
- a practical tool for stackable pathways into AI infrastructure support, GPU operations, SRE, platform operations, and related technical roles

## Best Proposal Theme
Recommended theme:
- Build a short-term, workforce-aligned AI infrastructure operations pathway at a community college using Aegis-GPU as the simulation, incident-training, and assessment layer.

## Suggested College-Facing Value Proposition
A community college lead can use Aegis to say:
- the program is not just teaching theory
- learners are practicing realistic AI infrastructure incident reasoning
- the pathway includes measurable progress and readiness evidence
- the training aligns with emerging workforce demand around GPU and AI infrastructure operations

## Recommended Next Action
Do not start by submitting directly as a standalone platform company.

Start by finding:
- one community college
- or one statewide/community college system contact
- or one workforce-development intermediary already working with a college lead

Then pitch Aegis as:
- the technical training layer
- the simulation environment
- the assessment and evidence engine

## Practical Assessment
If you can secure the right college partner quickly, SCC6 is the best current live target I found.
If you cannot secure that type of lead applicant, this is still useful as partnership outreach language, but not as a direct near-term application path.
