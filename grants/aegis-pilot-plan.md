# Aegis Pilot Plan

## Pilot Goal
Demonstrate that Aegis improves AI infrastructure troubleshooting readiness for learners preparing for GPU operations, platform engineering, SRE, or technical support roles.

## Pilot Structure

### Pilot Audience
Recommended starting cohorts:
- technical students in AI/HPC or systems programs
- workforce-development learners entering infrastructure roles
- junior operators or support staff in GPU-enabled environments

Ideal first pilot size:
- 15 to 40 learners

## Pilot Duration
Recommended pilot window:
- 6 to 8 weeks

## Proposed Pilot Phases

### Phase 1: Setup And Baseline
Duration:
- 1 week

Activities:
- participant onboarding
- baseline assessment
- environment orientation
- capture starting confidence and role background

Outputs:
- baseline assessment data
- participant roster
- pilot schedule

### Phase 2: Guided Training
Duration:
- 3 to 5 weeks

Activities:
- assign study guide and guided lab sequence
- run learners through incident families across hardware, fabric, runtime, storage, and orchestration
- collect progress, quiz, and reasoning-report artifacts

Suggested content path:
- GPU foundations and CUDA stack
- ECC and XID fault response
- NCCL and fabric path validation
- storage starvation and data path reasoning
- scheduler and orchestration incidents

Outputs:
- completion data
- reasoning progress exports
- quiz results
- representative branch-path evidence

### Phase 3: Assessment And Reporting
Duration:
- 1 to 2 weeks

Activities:
- endline scenario assessment
- compare baseline vs endline
- summarize learning patterns and readiness signals

Outputs:
- pilot summary report
- before/after metrics
- lessons learned
- recommendations for next release or curriculum iteration

## Success Criteria
The pilot should be considered successful if it demonstrates most of the following:
- strong participant completion rate
- measurable improvement in diagnostic accuracy
- measurable reduction in unsafe or over-broad actions
- evidence that learners better distinguish hardware, runtime, fabric, and storage failure classes
- positive instructor or reviewer feedback on realism and training value

## Required Operational Work
To support a real pilot, Aegis should prepare:
- stable pilot deployment
- clear onboarding instructions
- facilitator guide
- exported evidence summaries per learner or cohort
- issue log for pilot friction and content gaps

## Risks
Main pilot risks:
- over-scoping the first cohort
- mixing too many audience types in one pilot
- measuring only quiz scores instead of troubleshooting behavior
- claiming workforce impact before collecting baseline and endline evidence

## Best-Fit Pilot Narrative For Grants
The strongest grant-facing pilot narrative is:
- Aegis will run a structured, measurable pilot that improves AI infrastructure incident reasoning for emerging operators and technical learners, with outcome tracking focused on diagnostic accuracy, action safety, and readiness growth.
