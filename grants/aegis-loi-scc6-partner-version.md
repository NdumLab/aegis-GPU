# Aegis LOI Draft For SCC6 Partner Outreach

## Use Case
This version is not written as a direct submission from Aegis alone. It is written as a partnership draft for a community college or college system that could lead an SCC6 application.

Subject: Partnership concept for Strengthening Community Colleges Training Grants Round 6

Dear [Name],

I am reaching out because I believe [Institution] could be a strong lead applicant for the U.S. Department of Labor’s Strengthening Community Colleges Training Grants Round 6 opportunity, and that Aegis-GPU could serve as a differentiated training and assessment partner within that application.

Aegis-GPU is a browser-based AI infrastructure incident training and troubleshooting-readiness platform designed to help learners build practical operator judgment across GPU hardware, CUDA stack, networking, storage, and orchestration scenarios. Rather than focusing only on content delivery or command recall, Aegis emphasizes evidence-first reasoning, action safety, fault-layer identification, and consequence-aware troubleshooting practice.

This aligns well with SCC6’s focus on high-quality short-term training, workforce-system integration, and portable, stackable pathways tied to employer demand. Aegis can support a community-college-led pathway in AI infrastructure operations, platform support, GPU operations, or adjacent technical workforce preparation by providing:
- realistic incident-training modules
- guided study and quiz surfaces
- measurable reasoning and completion evidence
- browser-based delivery suitable for pilot deployment

Our current platform already includes validated guided labs, reasoning scorecards, progress reporting, and representative browser-proof evidence across key product flows. We believe this would allow [Institution] to propose a stronger training model for emerging technical roles tied to AI infrastructure and distributed systems operations.

The concept would be for [Institution] to serve as the lead applicant and academic/workforce pathway owner, with Aegis-GPU supporting the training environment, curriculum implementation, and learner-readiness evidence layer. This could be framed as a short-term, stackable technical pathway aligned with employer demand and supported by measurable pilot outcomes.

If this is of interest, I would welcome a short conversation about whether Aegis could strengthen an SCC6 concept at [Institution]. I can also share a one-page summary, pilot outline, and grant-ready positioning materials to make evaluation easier.

Thank you for your time.
