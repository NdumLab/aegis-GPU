# Aegis Budget Framework

## Purpose
This is a draft budget narrative framework for grant applications. It is meant to be adapted to a specific funding opportunity, not submitted unchanged.

## Budget Categories

### 1. Product Development
Use for:
- continued frontend and backend engineering
- pilot-reporting features
- outcome-tracking improvements
- stability and validation work

Rationale:
- Aegis is already a working platform, but pilot delivery requires stronger reporting, measured outcomes, and product hardening.

### 2. Curriculum And Content Development
Use for:
- incident scenario authoring
- facilitator guides
- study-guide refinement
- assessment design and rubric development

Rationale:
- the product value depends on realistic, well-structured training content tied to operator reasoning.

### 3. Evaluation And Reporting
Use for:
- pilot assessment design
- data analysis
- reporting
- outcome interpretation

Rationale:
- grant-funded pilots need measurable evidence, not only product activity logs.

### 4. Pilot Delivery And Support
Use for:
- onboarding sessions
- learner support
- instructor/facilitator time
- pilot coordination

Rationale:
- pilot success depends on structured delivery and participant support, especially for workforce-development settings.

### 5. Infrastructure And Hosting
Use for:
- hosting
- deployment operations
- logging and monitoring
- browser-proof validation environments

Rationale:
- the platform must remain stable and accessible throughout the pilot period.

## Example Narrative Language
Funds will support the conversion of Aegis from a validated prototype into a pilot-ready AI infrastructure training platform. Budgeted work includes product hardening, scenario and curriculum refinement, structured pilot delivery, and evaluation activities needed to measure improvements in troubleshooting readiness.

## Suggested Founder-Written Budget Priorities
If the grant is modest, prioritize:
- product hardening
- pilot evaluation
- curriculum packaging

If the grant is larger, add:
- facilitator capacity
- durable reporting infrastructure
- broader content coverage

## Cost Model Guidance
When adapting this framework to a real grant:
- separate one-time build costs from pilot delivery costs
- distinguish direct labor from infrastructure costs
- keep evaluation visible as its own line, not buried under engineering
- avoid inflated hosting claims unless they are actually required

## What Not To Do
- do not budget around a vague “AI platform” label
- do not make marketing the primary expense bucket
- do not present unsupported large-scale deployment assumptions
- do not claim robust cohort analytics if the current implementation has not yet been expanded to support them

## Best Near-Term Use
This framework is best used for:
- workforce-development grants
- educational innovation grants
- technical training and reskilling grants
- pilot and curriculum development proposals
